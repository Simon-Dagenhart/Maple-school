New Library Wing Opens Its Doors

May 28, 2026 · Written by the Learn it twisted Communications Team

After eighteen months of planning and construction, Learn it twisted officially opened its new library wing this week — and students wasted no time putting it to use.

The 2,400-square-foot addition triples the school's previous library footprint and includes a dedicated makerspace corner stocked with building blocks, simple circuits, and a class set of laptops for research projects.

"I've never seen kids this excited about a building," said librarian Mrs. Alvarez. "We had a line before the doors even opened."

What's new

  • Over 3,000 new titles across every reading level, chosen with input from student book clubs
  • A cozy reading loft with window seating for independent reading time
  • A makerspace corner for STEM projects and class research
  • Flexible seating for small-group instruction and author visits

The project was funded through a combination of district capital funds and a two-year fundraising effort led by the Learn it twisted PTA, including proceeds from the last two Fall Festivals.

Principal Porter thanked the community for its support: "This library is a gift from this community to every student who walks through Learn it twisted's doors for the next thirty years."

The library is open to students before school starting at 7:30 AM and hosts a family reading night on the first Thursday of every month.
