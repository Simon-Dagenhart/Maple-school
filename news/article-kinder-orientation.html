Kindergarten Orientation Day a Big Hit With Future Foxes

April 22, 2026 · Written by the Learn it twisted Communications Team

More than 70 incoming kindergartners and their families packed the cafeteria and classrooms this week for Learn it twisted's annual Kindergarten Orientation Day.

Families rotated through short stations led by current kindergarten teachers: a story-time circle, a "meet the classroom" walkthrough, and a Q&A session covering everything from drop-off routines to what to pack for lunch.

"We want kids walking into their first real day already knowing where their cubby is and what their teacher's voice sounds like," said kindergarten teacher Ms. Delgado. "It takes so much of the nervousness out of day one."

What families learned

  • Daily schedules, drop-off and pick-up procedures, and bus registration
  • What to expect during the first two weeks of school (a gradual, half-day start)
  • How to reach their child's teacher and the school office
  • Supply lists and how to sign up for the PTA's family directory

Each child went home with a small welcome bag, including a book to read over the summer and a card from their soon-to-be classmates.

Families who missed this session can request a one-on-one classroom visit by contacting the front office before the first day of school on August 24.
